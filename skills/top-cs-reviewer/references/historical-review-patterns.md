# Historical public-review patterns

Evidence boundary: 25 public ICLR 2020–2023 flows indexed from the Re²-derived public set, containing 94 reviews, 25 meta-reviews, and both accepted and rejected outcomes. These are behavioral examples only. Current 2026 official guides define policy and review-form requirements; this material must not be presented as WWW, ICML, or ICLR 2026 policy.

## Recurrent concern clusters

A conservative keyword-assisted audit found recurring concern families across the public reviews: contribution/originality positioning, clarity, comparator adequacy, reproducibility/implementation detail, theory/assumptions, related work, robustness, and limitations. The promoted pattern still uses the 10 distinct semantically reviewed papers spanning different decisions (`historical-iclr-review-concern-clusters`); five newly indexed flows do not automatically expand the rule.

Apply the pattern as follows:

1. Identify the central claim threatened by the concern.
2. Point to the exact evidence, assumption, comparator, or missing artifact.
3. Explain why the issue is blocking, major, minor, or not assessable.
4. Ask only for information that could change that classification.

## What not to infer

- Do not infer a causal relationship between a review phrase, score, rebuttal behavior, and acceptance.
- Do not copy reviewer prose or create a phrasebank.
- Do not treat historical rating scales as the current form.
- Do not transfer ICLR-specific behavioral evidence to WWW or ICML without an official or venue-specific source.

Use the selected venue's static reviewer overlay for the actual dimensions. Source IDs and thresholds are in `evidence/derived/rules.yaml` in the source repository and `_shared/evidence/derived/rules.yaml` after installation.
