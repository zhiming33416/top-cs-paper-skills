# Partial-manuscript protocol

When the input is incomplete:

1. List exactly which sections and artifacts were supplied.
2. Separate `visible defect` from `not shown in supplied scope`.
3. Review local claims fully, but qualify cross-section consistency, novelty, reproducibility, and policy conclusions.
4. Use `not assessable` for evidence that could exist outside the supplied material.
5. Request only artifacts that would materially change a high-priority finding.

Scope examples:

- **Abstract only:** assess claim clarity, apparent scope, result specificity, and overclaiming; do not conclude that experiments or citations are absent.
- **Figures/results only:** assess whether visible evidence supports stated captions or supplied claims; do not infer method correctness.
- **Section only:** inspect local logic and references, while marking full-paper consistency and venue compliance as unassessed.
- **Manuscript without supplement/code:** review main-paper self-containment and mark reproducibility details outside scope rather than missing.

Never reward or penalize a paper for material the user did not provide unless the venue requires it in the supplied artifact.
