# Response patterns

Use these patterns only with supplied facts.

## Valid concern with verified revision

`We agree that [specific issue]. We have [verified action]. The revision appears in [verified location]: [excerpt if supplied]. This change [bounded effect].`

## Clarification without a new experiment

`The requested quantity is already reported in [verified artifact]. We have revised [location] to make the connection explicit. The revised text now states [supplied text or placeholder].`

## Impossible or out-of-scope experiment

`We agree that [experiment] would test [claim]. It requires [true design dependency] beyond the present study. We therefore [available analysis or clarification] and narrow [claim] in [location]. The remaining limitation is [boundary].`

Do not cite time, cost, or convenience as the sole reason.

## Conflicting reviewers

Preserve both IDs, identify the shared objective and incompatible actions, follow any editor priority, choose one manuscript treatment consistent with the central claim, and explain the tradeoff separately to each reviewer.

## Missing evidence

Do not draft a factual result. Mark `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED` and request the minimum fields needed to complete the response.
