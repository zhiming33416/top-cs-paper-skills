# Historical public response/discussion patterns

Evidence boundary: 25 public ICLR 2020–2023 flows with 32 indexed discussion threads from the Re²-derived public set. Use this material for response behavior and state tracking only. Current official venue guides determine 2026 protocol, visibility, revision permissions, and limits. Because venue-specific promotion requires 30 distinct flows, ICLR behavior remains historical cross-case evidence rather than a promoted 2026 venue mode.

## Evidence anchors

Responses across the historical sample repeatedly anchor answers to concrete tables, figures, sections, equations, or supplied additional analyses. Promoted evidence spans 10 distinct papers (`historical-iclr-response-artifact-anchors`).

Require each factual answer to point to a supplied artifact. Use `[LOCATION NEEDED]` when the author has not provided a verified location. Keep planned analysis distinct from completed analysis.

## Stateful discussion

All indexed flows contain author response and reviewer follow-up; several contain additional rounds. Preserve comment IDs, prior answers, follow-up questions, and unresolved status across messages (`historical-iclr-response-discussion-is-stateful`). Do not regenerate each reply as an isolated letter.

## Disagreement

Evidence-based disagreement appears across at least six distinct papers (`historical-iclr-evidence-based-disagreement`). Use this sequence:

1. Restate the concern fairly.
2. Give a direct answer.
3. Point to verifiable evidence or a defensible scope boundary.
4. Record the manuscript clarification or limitation.

Do not claim that disagreement, new analysis, or response length caused acceptance. Do not reuse distinctive response wording. Full source IDs are in `evidence/derived/rules.yaml` in the source repository and `_shared/evidence/derived/rules.yaml` after installation.
