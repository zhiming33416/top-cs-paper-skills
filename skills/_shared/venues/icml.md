# ICML 2026

Last verified: 2026-07-06

## Official hard constraints

Source: https://icml.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorInstructions

- Evidence level: `official-policy`.
- Use the official LaTeX style; ICML does not support other typesetting systems for submission.
- Keep the main body within 8 pages, followed by any number of reference and appendix pages in one PDF.
- Keep the submission at or below 50 MB and preserve double-blind anonymity in the paper, appendix, code, data, and linked resources.
- Appendices may be part of the main PDF; supplementary code/data must also be anonymous.
- Do not make the camera-ready paper essentially different from the reviewed submission.

## Official review dimensions

Source: https://icml.cc/Conferences/2026/ReviewerInstructions

Evaluate soundness, presentation, significance, and originality separately. Ask 3–5 key questions only when answers could change the assessment or resolve a critical limitation. Check limitations, negative societal impact, reproducibility, ethics, and whether claims follow from proofs or experiments.

For an actual ICML review, follow the reviewer-specific LLM policy shown in the Reviewer Console; do not assume AI assistance is permitted.

## Soft conventions

Evidence level: `accepted-title-verified-public-version`; 30 unique ICML 2026 public versions after SHA deduplication, spanning Oral, Spotlight, and Regular. Paper-type and topic labels remain candidates pending human confirmation.

- A gap → approach → evidence abstract chain and explicit contribution preview recur across tiers.
- A dedicated related-work section was detected in 25/30 sources; treat this as a strong convention, not an official requirement.
- Keep soundness, significance, originality, and empirical benefit distinguishable so each can be audited.
- Treat public versions as structural evidence only. Use the official guide and template for format and submission requirements.
