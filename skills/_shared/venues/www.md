# WWW 2026 research track

Last verified: 2026-07-06

## Official hard constraints

Source: https://www2026.thewebconf.org/calls/research-tracks.html

- Evidence level: `official-policy`.
- Use English, the ACM double-column template, and `\documentclass[sigconf, anonymous, review]{acmart}` for review submission.
- Keep the main paper within 8 pages. References and an optional appendix may extend the single PDF to at most 12 pages total.
- Make the first 8 pages self-contained; reviewers are not required to read beyond them.
- Preserve double-blind anonymity and describe the authors' prior work in the third person.
- State relevance to the Web and the selected track clearly on the first page. Merely using a Web dataset, API, or social network is insufficient and can cause desk rejection.
- Treat the rebuttal as a non-interactive author response considered by reviewers and chairs.

## Author-visible evaluation criteria

Source: the same official research-track call.

Use the public Research call's criteria: technical merit, originality, potential impact, execution quality, presentation, related work, reproducibility, ethics, Web relevance, and track fit. No public WWW reviewer form is available in the verified source set; do not invent or import one.

## Soft conventions

Evidence level: `accepted-title-verified-public-version`; 30 verified WWW 2026 Research public versions. Industry, Short, and Web4Good remain indexed but excluded from Research style promotion. Paper-type and topic labels remain candidates pending human confirmation.

- The earlier semantic contribution/evidence observation remains supported by only three manually reviewed sources and stays low confidence.
- A dedicated related-work heading was detected in 23/30 sources and experiments/evaluation in 24/30; do not turn either observation into a mandatory section rule.
- Explicit first-page Web relevance remains an official hard rule, not a corpus-derived convention.
- Treat public versions as structural evidence only. Use the official Research guide and ACM template for submission format.
