# ICLR 2026

Last verified: 2026-07-06

## Official hard constraints

Source: https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuide

- Evidence level: `official-policy`.
- Use the official ICLR 2026 LaTeX style and double-blind anonymization.
- Limit the submission main text to 9 pages; references are excluded and appendices may follow the bibliography. Reviewers are not required to read appendices.
- During discussion/rebuttal and camera-ready, the main-text allowance is 10 pages.
- Submit supplementary text with the paper or separately, and anonymize all supplementary artifacts.
- Include an Ethics Statement when the work raises relevant concerns; the optional statement is outside the main-text limit and must not exceed one page.
- A paragraph-length Reproducibility Statement is strongly encouraged before references and is outside the main-text limit.
- Keep the submitted abstract genuine and informative because it is used for reviewer bidding.

## Official review perspective

Source: https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026/ReviewerGuide

Require a faithful contribution summary, comprehensive strengths and weaknesses, a justified recommendation, supporting arguments, decision-relevant questions, and constructive improvement feedback. Audit whether the evidence actually substantiates the claimed contribution.

## Soft conventions

Evidence level: `accepted-title-verified-public-version`; 30 unique ICLR 2026 public versions, spanning Oral and Poster. Paper-type and topic labels remain candidates pending human confirmation.

- A compact gap → approach → evidence abstract chain recurs but is not universal; use it as a reasoning pattern, not a template.
- Explicit contribution previews recur in introductions; connect each contribution to evidence rather than a component list.
- A related-work heading was detected in 26/30 sources after combining text headings and PDF outlines; integrated positioning also occurs. Do not force one placement.
- Treat public versions as structural evidence only. Use the official guide and template for page, format, anonymity, and appendix requirements.
