# Argument graph

Represent a full paper as a directed graph before drafting or auditing it:

- `problem -> gap -> central claim` records why the contribution is needed.
- `central claim -> supporting claims` separates the paper's main case from modules or observations.
- `claim -> evidence -> boundary` records the direct support and tested scope.
- `claim -> figure/table/theorem/citation` gives each important artifact a reasoning job.
- `claim -> section` prevents incompatible wording across title, abstract, introduction, results, and conclusion.

Flag orphan claims, unused evidence, circular support, and decisive evidence placed only in optional material. Do not manufacture an edge when the supplied artifacts do not establish it.
