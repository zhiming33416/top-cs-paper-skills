# Evidence discipline

Treat author-provided material as the only source of scientific facts unless the user explicitly authorizes literature retrieval.

## Non-negotiable rules

- Never invent a result, statistic, sample size, baseline, citation, mechanism, implementation detail, reviewer request, or completed experiment.
- Mark an unsupported statement as `[EVIDENCE NEEDED]`, `[CITATION NEEDED]`, `[DETAIL NEEDED]`, or `[AUTHOR DECISION]`.
- Preserve the difference between observed evidence, author interpretation, and speculation.
- Match claim strength to evidence strength. Prefer *suggests*, *is consistent with*, or a bounded claim when causality or generality is not established.
- Keep negative, null, and mixed results when they affect the central claim.
- Do not infer novelty from the absence of references in the supplied text.
- When editing, preserve citation-to-claim alignment and report any citation moved across claims.

## Source hierarchy

1. Official venue policy: hard constraint.
2. Author data, manuscript, figures, and code: scientific evidence.
3. Verified main-track papers: soft writing convention only.
4. Companion papers, preprints, and unverified papers: comparison or counterexample only.

Label uncertainty rather than hiding it.
