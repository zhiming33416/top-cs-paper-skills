# Style diagnostics

Diagnose before revising. Report only failures that materially affect comprehension, evidence, or venue fit.

| Failure | Diagnostic | Repair boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Empty prestige language | Adjectives replace a measurable distinction. | Replace with supplied capability or delete. |
| Nominalization chain | The actor and operation are hidden in nouns. | Restore a concrete subject and verb without changing agency. |
| Stacked modifiers | A reader cannot recover which noun each modifier controls. | Split or define; preserve technical compound terms. |
| Contribution inventory | Components are listed without claims or evidence. | Recast as contribution-evidence pairs. |
| Result/interpretation fusion | Observation and explanation appear as one fact. | Separate them and hedge the explanation. |
| Asymmetric comparison | Current work gets detail while prior work is caricatured. | Compare under matched assumptions and evidence. |
| Scope drift | Title, abstract, and conclusion use broader populations, tasks, or regimes than experiments. | Narrow wording to the tested scope. |
| Citation drift | A citation moves to support a different claim during editing. | Restore alignment or mark verification needed. |
| Generic transition | Connectors such as `Moreover` hide the actual logical relation. | State cause, contrast, condition, or consequence explicitly. |
| Terminology variation | Synonyms are used for the same technical object. | Enforce the terminology ledger. |

Do not impose a universal sentence-length cap. Split a sentence when dependency depth, mixed logical roles, or ambiguity makes it hard to verify.
