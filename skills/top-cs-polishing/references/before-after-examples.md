# Before-and-after patterns

Use the transformations, not the wording.

## Unsupported strength

Before: `Our method comprehensively solves domain shift.`

After pattern: `Under [tested shifts], the method [supplied result] relative to [comparators].`

Reason: replace a universal claim with tested scope and direct evidence.

## Component inventory

Before: `Our contributions are a new encoder, loss, and training strategy.`

After pattern: `We introduce [mechanism] to address [bottleneck]; [experiment/theorem] tests whether it improves [claim].`

Reason: connect technical moves to claims and evidence.

## Observation versus mechanism

Before: `Performance increased, proving that the module learns causal structure.`

After pattern: `Performance increased by [supplied value] under [condition]. This result is consistent with, but does not establish, the proposed causal explanation.`

## Compression

Before: two paragraphs repeat motivation and the same three numeric results.

After procedure: keep one motivation sentence, preserve every number with its condition and comparator, then state one bounded interpretation. Record any removed citation or qualifier in the ledger.

## Chinese-structured English

Before pattern: background, author opinion, several connectives, then the actual result.

After procedure: lead with the controlling claim, place evidence immediately after it, preserve hedge and comparison direction, and end with interpretation or boundary.
