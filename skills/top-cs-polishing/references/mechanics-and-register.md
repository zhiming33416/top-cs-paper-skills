# Mechanics and academic register

Provenance: `conservative-implementation`. Apply field conventions and venue templates over generic language preferences.

## Articles and countability

- Introduce a singular countable object with an article or determiner: `a model`, `the estimator`, `this dataset`.
- Use `the` for a uniquely defined object in the local context, not every technical noun.
- Distinguish a method class from an instance: `contrastive learning` versus `the contrastive objective used here`.
- Preserve established names that conventionally omit an article.

## Agreement and reference

- Match verbs to the grammatical head, not the nearest noun.
- Make pronoun antecedents explicit when multiple models, datasets, or equations are available.
- Treat `data` according to the target field's established style, but stay consistent.
- Avoid `this` or `these results` when the referenced result is ambiguous.

## Tense and aspect

- Use present tense for paper structure, definitions, equations, and generally valid statements.
- Use past tense for completed experimental actions and observed outcomes when natural.
- Use present perfect only when connecting a verified body of prior work to the present question.
- Keep proposed mechanism distinct from observed result through modal or evidential language.

Do not enforce a section-wide tense mechanically; choose tense by information status.

## Voice and agency

Use active voice when the actor matters and passive voice when the process or object is the focus. Avoid hiding responsibility for dataset filtering, annotation, model selection, or excluded runs. Do not rewrite conventional mathematical passive constructions solely to satisfy a style rule.

## Numbers, units, and symbols

- Preserve exact numeric value, sign, unit, scale, and comparison direction.
- Keep spacing and capitalization consistent with the governing template and SI conventions where applicable.
- Define metric arrows or direction in tables.
- Use consistent decimal precision within comparable columns unless precision conveys measurement resolution.
- Distinguish percentage points from percent change.
- Do not convert units or round values without reporting the change.

## Abbreviations

- Define at first meaningful use and use one canonical form afterward.
- Do not abbreviate a term used only a few times unless the field convention requires it.
- Preserve capitalization-sensitive model, dataset, and metric names.
- Check title and abstract separately when venue style treats them as standalone text.

## Sentence architecture

Split when a sentence contains multiple claims with different evidence, deeply nested conditions, or an ambiguous attachment. Merge short sentences only when their logical relation becomes clearer. Do not impose a universal word limit.

Prefer concrete subjects and finite verbs. Retain nominal forms when they are established technical concepts. Keep conditions next to the claim they qualify and comparisons next to their reference set.

## Register

Replace promotional evaluation with measurable description. Remove conversational fillers, excessive metadiscourse, and claims about reader reaction. Preserve direct first-person `we` when allowed and useful; do not alternate between `we`, `the authors`, and passive voice for stylistic variety.

## Final mechanics pass

Check terminology, abbreviation definitions, symbols, units, number preservation, citations, labels, cross-references, punctuation around equations, and template-specific capitalization. Mechanics correction must not change scientific scope.
