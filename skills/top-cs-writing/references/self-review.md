# Adversarial self-review

Provenance: `conservative-implementation`; use official venue dimensions where available and do not predict acceptance.

## Argument audit

- Can the full paper be reduced to one falsifiable central claim?
- Does the gap follow from verified prior-work positioning?
- Does every supporting claim connect to direct evidence and a boundary?
- Are any artifacts orphaned or any claims unsupported?
- Do title, abstract, introduction, experiments, and conclusion preserve compatible scope?

## Evidence audit

- Is decisive evidence in the required main-paper boundary?
- Are comparators fair under information, tuning, and compute?
- Are uncertainty, negative results, and failures visible?
- Are causal, generality, efficiency, and novelty claims matched to appropriate tests?
- Are planned and completed analyses clearly separated?

## Reader audit

- Can a reviewer identify relevance, distinction, trust basis, reuse path, and boundary?
- Are terms locked and defined before use?
- Does each paragraph have one controlling job?
- Do transitions state the actual relation rather than use generic connectors?

## Venue and integrity audit

- Use the current official profile for page limits, anonymity, appendices, and stage-specific permissions.
- Check author identity, repository URLs, metadata, acknowledgements, and self-citation treatment.
- Do not infer policy from accepted-paper formatting.
- Mark unsupported citations, results, and manuscript locations explicitly.

## Return a blocking list

Rank no more than five issues by threatened claim and consequence. Label each `text-only`, `analysis-needed`, `experiment-needed`, `proof-needed`, `policy/compliance`, or `author-decision`. Do not replace the requested draft with an unlimited checklist.
