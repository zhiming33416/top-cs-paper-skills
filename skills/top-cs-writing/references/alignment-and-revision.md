# Alignment and targeted revision

Use an alignment checkpoint only when a wrong assumption would waste a long draft. Show the one-sentence central argument, paragraph jobs, target venue/audience, terminology collisions, inferred contribution or lead result, and at most two questions that materially change the draft.

Do not stop for confirmation when the user supplied a clear claim, evidence, boundary, and deliverable.

When revising after feedback:

1. Identify the exact claim, paragraph, or structural relation rejected by the author.
2. Preserve unaffected text verbatim where possible.
3. State when a local correction forces a wider change to terminology, evidence mapping, or section order.
4. Update the terminology ledger and claim map before propagating the correction.
5. Re-run only checks affected by the edit, then report changed and preserved regions.

If the feedback invalidates the central argument, return to planning rather than patching fluent prose onto a broken premise.
