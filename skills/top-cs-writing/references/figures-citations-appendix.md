# Figures, citations, and appendix planning

- Assign every planned figure or table a question, claim, comparator, and expected reading order. Use `[FIGURE NEEDED: purpose]` or `[TABLE NEEDED: purpose]` when the artifact does not exist.
- Place the first decisive artifact near the claim it supports. Do not cite a future artifact as completed evidence.
- Use `[CITATION NEEDED: claim]` instead of inventing a reference. Keep citation clusters attached to the exact proposition they support.
- Keep central definitions, claims, assumptions, and decisive evidence in the main paper. Move derivations, exhaustive examples, extended results, and implementation detail only when the venue permits it and the main claim remains assessable.
- Record appendix dependencies in the argument graph and flag any main-text sentence that cannot be evaluated without optional material.
