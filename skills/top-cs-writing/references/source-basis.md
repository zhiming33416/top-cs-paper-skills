# Source basis

Use `../_shared/rule-provenance.yaml` to interpret labels.

## Directly grounded rules

- **Official policy:** venue/year limits, anonymity, appendix boundaries, WWW first-page relevance, and submission-stage constraints come from the selected verified venue profile.
- **Corpus-derived:** the recurring abstract gap-approach-evidence chain, explicit contribution previews, related-work placement frequencies, and section-heading prevalence come from promoted rules in `evidence/derived/rules.yaml` and `full-section-rules.yaml`.
- **Author artifact:** every scientific fact, result, claim, citation relation, method detail, and novelty distinction must come from user-supplied material or explicitly authorized retrieval.

## Conservative implementation choices

The argument graph, claim-evidence-boundary table, terminology ledger, reader workflow, paragraph-job map, alignment checkpoint, and targeted-revision protocol are repository workflow choices. They improve traceability but are not claimed to be mandatory venue structures or empirically associated with acceptance.

The detailed section guides and synthetic patterns describe defensible ways to execute those choices. Treat them as selectable patterns, not sentences to imitate or universal templates.

## Unsupported conclusions

Do not infer that a structure caused acceptance, that an absent heading is disfavored, that a phrase is venue-preferred, or that a contribution is novel because it differs from the local corpus.
